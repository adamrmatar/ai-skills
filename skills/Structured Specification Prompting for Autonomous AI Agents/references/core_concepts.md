## Core Concepts
Structured specification prompting is a method that involves creating detailed, structured instructions for AI agents to autonomously complete complex tasks. This approach contrasts with basic prompting, where the user provides minimal instructions and often needs to iterate multiple times to achieve the desired result.

### Key Elements
1. **Task Definition**: Clearly define the task you want the AI to complete. This includes specifying the end goal and any constraints.
2. **Task Decomposition**: Break down the task into smaller, manageable components. This helps the AI understand the structure and requirements of the task.
3. **Requirement Specification**: Detail the requirements for each component. This includes content, format, and any other relevant details.
4. **Formatting Guidelines**: Provide specific formatting instructions to ensure consistency and professionalism in the output.

### Benefits
- **Efficiency**: Structured specifications reduce the need for multiple iterations, saving time.
- **Accuracy**: Detailed instructions lead to more accurate and complete outputs.
- **Autonomy**: AI agents can complete tasks with minimal supervision, freeing up your time for other activities.
