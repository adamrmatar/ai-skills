## Common Pitfalls
When creating structured specifications for AI agents, there are several common pitfalls to avoid.

### Overloading the Prompt
Including too much information in the prompt can confuse the AI. Focus on essential details and avoid unnecessary complexity.

### Lack of Detail
Insufficient detail can result in incomplete or incorrect outputs. Ensure all requirements are specified, including content, format, and any other relevant details.

### Ambiguity
Ambiguous instructions can lead to inconsistent results. Be clear and precise in your specifications.

### Ignoring Feedback
Failing to use feedback from initial results to refine your specifications can result in suboptimal outputs. Iterate and improve your instructions based on the AI's performance.

### Example
```markdown
Poor Prompt: 'Create a PowerPoint deck on AI.'
Better Prompt: 'Create a PowerPoint deck on AI productivity trends. Include a title slide, introduction, key trends, case studies, and conclusion. Use a professional template with a blue color scheme.'
```

By avoiding these pitfalls, you can create effective structured specifications that enable AI agents to autonomously complete tasks with high accuracy and efficiency.
