# Academic Research with NotebookLM

## Overview
NotebookLM is a powerful tool for academic research, offering features like source management, mind mapping, literature review creation, and self-critique. This skill will guide you through the best practices for using NotebookLM to enhance your research workflows.

## Step-by-Step Workflow
1. **Create a New Notebook**: Start by creating a new notebook for each research task or paper. This helps keep your sources and notes organized.
2. **Add Sources**: Upload various types of sources, including PDFs, websites, YouTube videos, and voice memos. Use the 'Add Sources' feature to include everything relevant to your research.
3. **Create Mind Maps**: Use the 'Create Mind Map' feature to visualize the themes and knowledge gaps in your sources. Expand the mind map to identify areas lacking information.
4. **Generate Literature Reviews**: Ask NotebookLM specific questions about your sources to extract key information. Save these notes and convert them into sources for a comprehensive literature review.
5. **Self-Critique**: Upload your own writing and ask NotebookLM to critique it. Identify unsupported claims, logical gaps, and areas needing stronger citations.
6. **Visualize Data**: Use the 'Data Table' feature to create tables summarizing major findings from your sources.
7. **Create Visual Aids**: Generate slide decks, infographics, and flashcards to present your research findings.

## Code/Prompt Templates
```markdown
# Literature Review Prompt
What research methodologies are used across these studies? Compare their sample sizes, populations, and key limitations.

# Self-Critique Prompt
What arguments am I making in my draft? Are they well supported by the literature sources?

# Data Table Prompt
Create a data table summarizing the major findings in solar cell technology evolution.
```

## Best Practices
- **Source Strategy**: Don't just upload PDFs. Include diverse sources like YouTube videos and voice memos.
- **Mind Mapping**: Use mind maps to identify research gaps by looking for areas with sparse information.
- **Literature Reviews**: Break down your literature review into sections and generate summaries for each.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Overloading Sources**: Avoid uploading too many irrelevant sources. Focus on quality over quantity.
- **Ignoring Gaps**: Don't just focus on what's present in your sources. Look for what's missing to identify research gaps.

## Validation Steps
1. **Check Source Relevance**: Ensure all uploaded sources are directly relevant to your research.
2. **Review Mind Maps**: Verify that the mind map accurately represents the themes and gaps in your sources.
3. **Validate Literature Review**: Cross-check the generated literature review with your original sources for accuracy.
