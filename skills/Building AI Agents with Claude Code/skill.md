## Overview
Claude Code is a powerful tool for building AI agents that can automate tasks, manage workflows, and leverage local and online data. This skill will guide you through the process of setting up Claude Code, creating AI agents, and optimizing their performance through context engineering and iterative improvement.

## Core Concepts
Before diving into the workflow, it's essential to understand the core concepts:
- **AI Harness**: Claude Code acts as a harness that leverages AI models like Opus, Sonnet, or Haiku to perform tasks.
- **Context Engineering**: The process of feeding relevant context to your AI agent to improve its output.
- **Iterative Improvement**: Continuously refining your AI agent based on feedback and performance data.

For a deeper dive into these concepts, refer to [Core Concepts](references/core_concepts.md).

## Step-by-Step Workflow
1. **Install Claude Code**: Download and install the Claude Code desktop app or use it within VS Code.
2. **Set Up Your Environment**: Configure Claude Code to access your local files and online services like Gmail, Slack, and CRM.
3. **Create Your First AI Agent**: Use Claude Code to build a simple automation task, such as organizing your downloads folder.
4. **Context Engineering**: Feed relevant context to your AI agent, such as meeting transcripts, YouTube videos, and Slack threads.
5. **Iterative Improvement**: Continuously refine your AI agent based on feedback and performance data.
6. **Automate Complex Tasks**: Build more complex automations, such as drafting tailored responses to customer emails.

For detailed instructions and examples, refer to [Practical Guide](references/practical_guide.md).

## Best Practices
- **Start Simple**: Begin with straightforward tasks to get comfortable with Claude Code.
- **Feed Relevant Context**: Always provide your AI agent with the necessary context to perform its tasks effectively.
- **Iterate Continuously**: Regularly review and refine your AI agent based on feedback and performance data.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Overlooking Context**: Failing to provide sufficient context can lead to generic or irrelevant outputs.
- **Ignoring Feedback**: Not using feedback to improve your AI agent can result in stagnant performance.
- **Scope Creep**: Adding too many features at once can complicate your AI agent and reduce its effectiveness.

For more best practices and pitfalls, refer to [Common Pitfalls](references/common_pitfalls.md).

## Validation and Testing
1. **Test Simple Tasks**: Start by testing simple tasks to ensure your AI agent is functioning correctly.
2. **Review Outputs**: Regularly review the outputs of your AI agent to ensure they meet your expectations.
3. **Gather Feedback**: Collect feedback from users or stakeholders to identify areas for improvement.
4. **Refine and Iterate**: Use the feedback to refine your AI agent and improve its performance.

For detailed validation steps, refer to [Practical Guide](references/practical_guide.md).
